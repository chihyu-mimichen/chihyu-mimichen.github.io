Chih-Yu Chen
Research Associate / Doctoral Candidate
I am currently working as a research associate and pursuing my PhD at the Chair of Carbon Composites at the Technical University of Munich (TUM), specializing in developing the manufacturing process of continuous fiber non-planar 3D printing using KUKA robots. I'm also the founder of DASH, a student initiative at TUM dedicated to developing lower-limb exoskeletons for paraplegic patients. My skills include machine design, additive manufacturing, artificial intelligence, and finite element modeling.

Exoskeleton Research

The research and development of exoskeletons can be divided into multiple topics. Several topics, including motion prediction, obstacle recognition, and human-exoskeleton interaction, have been studied.

TUM DASH

Student team at the Technical University of Munich developing and building lower-limb exoskeletons for paraplegic patients.

Continuous Fiber Robotic 3D Printing

Continuous fiber printhead intergrated on a robotic arm for printing small and complex carbon fiber reinforced structures.

Slicer for Non-Planar 3D Printing

A computational framework that allows for the deposition of material on non-planar surfaces along the direction of the largest principal stress obtained from a finite element analysis following topology optimization.

Transparency Control of 1-DoF Exoskeleton Knee Joint

A novel approach to transparency control based on a human-in-the-loop velocity optimization framework.

Finite Element Modeling of Extreme Shape Transformation
