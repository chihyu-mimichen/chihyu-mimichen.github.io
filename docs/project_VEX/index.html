VEX Robotics Competition

The VEX Robotics Competition is a competition that inspires students worldwide to pursue STEM-related education and career paths. Each year, the competition revolves around a different theme, challenging high school students from all over the world to build robots capable of completeing different tasks. The theme of the VEX Robotics Competition competition in 2013 is called the "Sack Attack."

Below is an illustration depicting the competition's battlefield. Two alliances, one "red" and one "blue", each consisting of two teams, compete in each match. The primary objective of the match is to achieve a higher score by using the self-built robot to transport the bean-sacks (shown in green and yellow) from the original location to the scoring location, including the Floor Goals, Troughs, and High Goals. Additionally, points are awarded based on the robot's final parking location at the end of the match.

Photo credit: VEX Robotics


Our two robots, 4744A and 4744B, achieved remarkable success in the VEX regional competition in Tokyo, Japan in 2013. 4744B emerged as the tournament champion and excelled in the skills challenge, while 4744A received recognition with the design award, which led us to the VEX world championship in California, USA in the same year.
